Application snapshot.

Exact dates and links change each year. This page keeps the public expectations clear without publishing private setup details.

01

Status

When applications open

Applications are usually organized in late winter or early spring for a May-July summer cohort.

02

Fit

Who it is for

The program is built for Roxbury Community College students who can commit to the full 8-week schedule and are excited to learn in a research lab.

03

Time

Expected commitment

Typical hours are Monday-Thursday, 9am-2pm, for 8 weeks.

What students can expect.

The program is paid, mentored, and built around learning by doing. Students are supported through lab skills, scientific context, data analysis, and final communication.

1

No phage background required

Instructors start with core skills and build vocabulary through experiments.

2

Real research, not a demo

Students work with samples, plaques, strains, genomes, figures, and poster stories.

3

Mentors close by

Graduate students, postdocs, staff, and faculty help students troubleshoot and interpret results.

4

A public finish line

The summer ends with posters and, where ready, phage records that connect to public science.

Annual update note

Application links and exact dates should be updated each year once RCC and program leads confirm the next cohort timeline.
