Eight weeks from sample to poster.

The calendar changes each year, but the summer follows a practical weekly plan: safety and pipetting, environmental sampling, plaques, purification, titers, microscopy, DNA, genome comparisons, and final posters.

From field sampling to public explanation.

Students move from learning core techniques to producing data they can explain. Instructors teach from the same public protocols so handoffs and expectations stay consistent.

The day has a repeatable lab rhythm.

Students are usually on campus Monday-Thursday from 9am-2pm. Most days combine a short setup discussion, bench work or bioinformatics, notebook/data time, and a closeout conversation.

Morning

Frame the experiment

Connect the day’s protocol to host bacteria, phage biology, or the poster story students are building.

Midday

Run the work

Plate samples, pick plaques, prepare lysates, run gels, image phages, compare strains, or analyze sequence data.

Close

Record and interpret

Notebook entries, calculations, figure sketches, and group discussion keep the research story moving.

Every method points back to the isolate.

Students collect samples, make measurements, name discoveries, learn the language of phage biology, and build a scientific narrative from their own data.
