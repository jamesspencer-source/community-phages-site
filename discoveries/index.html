From sample to poster claim.

The scientific output is more than a name. Students learn how to turn observations into figures, claims, comparisons, and a presentation for a real audience.

1

Environmental sample

Students collect soil or water and document where each sample came from.

2

Plaque evidence

Clearings on bacterial lawns become the first visible hint of phage activity.

3

Genome context

Sequencing and bioinformatics connect each phage to relatives, genes, and hypotheses.

4

Poster story

Students assemble figures, practice explanations, and present what they learned.

What gets published

Discovery pages should highlight phages, methods, figures, poster claims, and public records. Student records and internal planning materials stay in program systems.
