A program built year by year.

Community Phages began as a discovery-based summer program and now includes an 8-week RCC-HMS internship, public protocols, instructor teams, PhagesDB records, partner visits, and student poster sessions.

Program history.

Prior program pages are summarized here so the site keeps institutional memory as the program changes year to year.

2022

Pilot year

The program began as a discovery-based summer research experience inspired by SEA-PHAGES and adapted for RCC students working at HMS.

2023

Expanded instructor network

The teaching team broadened across HMS labs, adding more phage defense, microbial evolution, and microscopy expertise.

2024

Deeper program structure

Public protocols, year-specific resources, instructor rotation, and student-facing materials became more formalized.

2025

Public phage collection

Student discoveries were prepared for public PhagesDB records, and poster/session practices became a stronger capstone.

2026

Current planning cycle

The program continues as an 8-week May-July research internship with field sampling, bioinformatics, partner visits, and a final poster session.

Diversity and equity mission.

Community Phages is designed to make an HMS research experience accessible to RCC students through paid time in the lab, close mentoring, clear protocols, and a teaching team that expects students to contribute real data.

Acknowledgments

The program was inspired by HHMI SEA-PHAGES, with protocols and materials adapted or reproduced with permission. The site also recognizes the HMS, RCC, sponsor, field-site, and mentor communities that keep the program running.
