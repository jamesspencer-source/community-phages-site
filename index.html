A research program built as a sequence of evidence.

Students do not rehearse a demonstration. They collect samples, test bacterial lawns, purify isolates, image phages, work with DNA, analyze sequence data, and build a poster from their own results.

From an environmental sample to a record that another scientist can read.

Each phage becomes a specimen with context: where it came from, how it infects a host, what it looks like by microscopy, what its genome encodes, and how it compares to known relatives.
